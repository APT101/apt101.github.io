Policy: Handling Phishing Emails

Clear steps on what to do when you receive a suspicious message. Left side: everyday internet users. Right side: corporate / workplace users.

For Home / Personal Users

1. When You Receive a Suspicious Email
  • Do not click any links or attachments.
  • Do not reply or interact with the sender.
  • Check the sender’s address and the message tone.
  • Verify using an official website or app, not the email link.
2. If You Clicked or Entered Information
  • Close the page immediately.
  • Change the password on the real site.
  • If reused anywhere, change it everywhere.
  • Turn on MFA (multi-factor authentication).
  • Run a full antivirus or malware scan.
3. Cleanup / Reporting
  • Use your email's “Report phishing” button if available.
  • Block the sender.
  • Delete the email once handled.

For Corporate / Workplace Users

1. When You Receive a Suspicious Email
  • Do not click links or open attachments.
  • Do not delete the email — security may need it.
  • Report using the company process:
    • “Report Phishing” button
    • SOC / IT security email
    • Helpdesk ticket
  • Include sender, subject, & why it looked suspicious.
2. If You Clicked or Entered Credentials
  • Disconnect from Wi-Fi or network immediately.
  • Notify IT/SOC right away.
  • Do not restart the system unless told to.
  • Follow IT’s remediation steps (reset passwords, revoke sessions, reimage devices).
3. Best Practices
  • Use only company-approved apps, email clients, and VPN.
  • Keep all systems updated.
  • Do not forward internal data to personal accounts.
  • Verify unusual requests (payments, gift cards, credentials) by phone or in person.
