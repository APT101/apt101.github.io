Glossary: Phishing & Email Security

Short, simple definitions of key terms used in phishing, cybercrime, and email security.

Phishing
A fraudulent attempt to steal information by pretending to be a trusted source.
Spear-phishing
A targeted phishing attack aimed at one specific person or group.
Whaling
Spear-phishing that targets executives or high-value employees.
Smishing
Phishing delivered through SMS/text messaging.
Vishing
Phishing through voice calls, pretending to be banks, IT support, etc.
Spoofing
Faking an email address, caller ID, or website so it appears legitimate.
Malware
Software designed to harm devices or steal data.
Payload
The malicious action triggered when malware runs (like encrypting files).
Credential Harvesting
Stealing usernames, passwords, or login codes through fake login pages.
Social Engineering
Manipulating people into doing actions that compromise security.
MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication)
Using two or more verification steps (password + code) to log in.
Zero-day
A vulnerability that has no fix available yet.
URL Redirect
A link that silently leads to a different website than what is displayed.
Sandboxing
Running suspicious files in a safe, isolated environment.
Threat Intelligence
Information about known malicious domains, IPs, malware, and adversaries.
SPF
A DNS record specifying which servers may send email for a domain.
DKIM
A cryptographic signature that ensures an email hasn't been altered.
DMARC
A policy telling mail servers how to handle messages that fail SPF/DKIM.
